We want to protect the joy, goodwill, and volunteer time of the maintainers and contributors of the Python Calendaring Ecosystem (PCE). As such, we take a strong stance against AI abuse.

Contributors to PCE must follow this AI policy as described below.

Responsible AI use 🔗

You may responsibly use AI as a tool to draft a pull request. That means you must comply with pull request requirements as defined by each project and follow PCE's Code of Conduct.

If you use AI in your work:

  • You must take responsibility for the output, including reviewing and validating it for accuracy and ensuring it resolves an issue.
  • You must check the AI's terms of use and ensure that outputs are not reconstructed from copyrighted sources.
  • You are expected to understand and be able to explain design and code decisions.
  • In your git commit messages, you must specify both (1) which AI model and version you used, and (2) how you used it, by either including the prompts and interactions or summarizing them.
  • You must disclose that you used AI in your change log entry.
  • You are accountable for your AI-generated content.

AI abuse 🔗

You may not abuse AI to generate a pull request that is disruptive to the PCE community or does not adhere to responsible AI use. Examples of abuse include:

  • Claiming no responsibility for the output of AI-generated content.
  • Submitting a pull request that demonstrates no understanding or thought toward solving the issue.
  • Plagiarising copyrighted or other material to which you have no legal claim.
  • Ignoring or failing to respond to feedback.
  • The GitHub account itself being an AI agent.

Report suspected violations 🔗

To report a suspected violation, see the Enforcement section in the Code of Conduct. Maintainers may close pull requests without feedback if they are deemed spam, AI slop, or abuse. Maintainers may also suspend, ban, or report GitHub users, as described in PCE's Code of Conduct.
